Case Study TripApp

TripApp is a travel planner that shows hotels, restaurant reviews, attractions, and other travel content. It also includes interactive chat that can be used between travelers.  Simple to you use, it can help you organize your trip day per day, communicate with your friends, and organize your friend’s ideas.

  • The problem: Traveling in groups is great but the organization can be very intimidating. Lack of communication, stress, overwhelmed, a lot of people will just follow the group anxious to have to deal with everyone’s ideas.
  • The solution: TripApp is developed to decrease this amount of stress by helping with the organization and the communication of group travelers into the app. You can chat with your friends, plan your trip, and vote for your friend’s ideas. Communication becomes then easier and everyone can now participate.
  • My Role: UX Designer (Individual Project)
  • Tools: Adobe XD, Photoshop, Miro, Unsplash, The noun project, Google, Illustrator

User Research

Proto-Persona

My first step of this User Research was to guess what type of user I will be design for. Who travels often and for who a travel planner will help? Creating a Proto-persona helped me empathy with someone that I imagined. I started to care about Logan’s needs and problems! It made me want to dig deeper.

Interviews

I interviewed 8 person. I went out in to talk to strangers, talked to my friends, and people at my work. With my interviews, I discovered that there is two type of users. People who travel for fun and people who travel for work.

User Flow

I started to sketch my User flow. It was very messy and not too clear but I had an idea of where I was going. It helped me define my main steps of the app.

Creating the user flow for “Create a trip” was hard because it’s a lot of possibilities and options. However, it’s also where my main focus on my app is which is: planning, communication, and voting ideas.

Wireframes

Using a sketch wireframe gave me more perspective of how my app is going to look like. I started visualizing my app and it made more sense to me. By sketching, I also had to change a few things from my user flow. It made sense on the user flow but not on the sketching.

This is where everything come to life. It was very pleasant to make the digital wireframes based on my sketches. I also changed a few things that made more sense on digital and I added a lot of things that I didn’t thought about while I was sketching the wireframes.

Testing

After testing my product to users and re-watch the tester videos, I collected the data and organized them by groups. Postives feedbacks, issues, friends functions, and navigation. I then, organized the users issuers into a prioritize matrix to understand what was the most important.

Final Thought

I realized that UX design focuses entirely on the user itself and their needs. We want to understand the user, understand the problem, feel the problem, and emphasize with the user. I thought that was very interesting and deep to create a persona with a picture and a personality because by creating this “person” you start to care more about the person’s problem. Like your kid. I also think that I could’ve done better with more time and more research. This project was a learning experience and made me realize that you can always do better.  The key is two be able to step out of your shoes and be open-minded.
